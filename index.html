About

I came to education data through an unlikely path. I started my career as a band director, spending years in the classroom before moving into educational compliance and data administration. That transition led me to pursue an Ed.D. in Education Leadership and Policy at UCLA, with additional doctoral coursework at UC Riverside in school finance, policy analysis, and advanced statistics. The through-line has always been the same: using evidence to make better decisions for students.

Today I lead a team at the Riverside County Office of Education that produces statewide data products used by districts across California. We build automated accountability reports for every local educational agency in the state, maintain Tableau dashboards that inform policy and practice, and manage data sharing partnerships with CSU and the State Treasurer's Office through the CalKIDS program. Our stack runs on Python, R, SQL, Kubernetes, Airflow, dbt, Tableau, and Shiny—but the technology is always in service of the question, not the other way around.

I was named RCOE's 2024 Classified Administrator of the Year, a recognition I share with the team that makes this work possible. When I'm not building data pipelines or debugging container orchestration, I'm usually at a conference talking about how public education agencies can modernize their data infrastructure without losing sight of the students at the center of every dataset.
